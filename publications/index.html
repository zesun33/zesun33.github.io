  1. IEEE TCDS
    Delving deeper into astromorphic transformers
    Md Zesun Ahmed Mia, Malyaban Bal, and Abhronil Sengupta
    IEEE Transactions on Cognitive and Developmental Systems, 2025
    Abs Bib

    This work presents astromorphic transformers, a bio-inspired neural architecture that leverages principles from astronomical observation patterns and cognitive processing mechanisms. We introduce novel attention mechanisms that mimic stellar formation dynamics and gravitational interactions to improve long-range dependency modeling in transformers. The proposed approach demonstrates significant improvements in computational efficiency and representational capacity across multiple cognitive and developmental tasks. Experimental results show enhanced performance in sequence modeling, pattern recognition, and adaptive learning scenarios, with reduced computational overhead compared to traditional transformer architectures. The astromorphic design principles offer new insights into scalable neural network architectures for cognitive developmental systems.

2024

  1. Matter
    Self-sensitizable neuromorphic device based on adaptive hydrogen gradient
    Tao Zhang, Mingjie Hu, Md Zesun Ahmed Mia, and 8 more authors
    Matter, 2024
    Abs Bib

    We report a novel self-sensitizable neuromorphic device that employs adaptive hydrogen gradients to achieve brain-like synaptic plasticity and learning functionality. The device architecture exploits controlled hydrogen migration and redistribution within nanostructured materials to modulate synaptic weights dynamically. Through systematic characterization, we demonstrate that the hydrogen gradient mechanism enables both short-term and long-term plasticity, essential for neuromorphic computing applications. The device exhibits remarkable endurance, low power consumption, and excellent retention characteristics. Our approach provides insights into the role of ionic species in artificial synaptic devices and opens new pathways for developing energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware with enhanced learning capabilities and biological fidelity.

  2. OpenReview
    RMAAT: A Bio-Inspired Approach for Efficient Long-Context Sequence Processing in Transformers
    Md Zesun Ahmed Mia, Malyaban Bal, and Abhronil Sengupta
    2024
    Abs Bib

    RMAAT presents a novel bio-inspired approach to address the computational challenges of processing long-context sequences in transformer architectures. Drawing inspiration from biological neural attention mechanisms and memory consolidation processes, we introduce a recursive memory-attention architecture that dynamically allocates computational resources based on sequence relevance and temporal importance. The proposed method employs adaptive attention pruning, hierarchical memory structures, and biologically-motivated forgetting mechanisms to achieve linear complexity scaling with sequence length. Extensive experiments on long-document understanding, video analysis, and temporal sequence modeling demonstrate significant improvements in both computational efficiency and performance accuracy. RMAAT reduces memory requirements by up to 80% while maintaining or improving task performance, enabling practical deployment of transformers on resource-constrained environments for long-context applications.

  3. iCACCESS
    Ultra Low Cost, Low Power, High Speed Electronic Braille Device for Visually Impaired People
    Md Zesun Ahmed Mia and Kazi Toukir Ahmed
    In 2024 International Conference on Advances in Computing, Communication, Electrical, and Smart Systems (iCACCESS), 2024
    Abs Bib

    This paper presents the design, fabrication, and evaluation of an ultra-low-cost, high-speed electronic Braille device specifically engineered for visually impaired individuals. The proposed system utilizes innovative actuator technology and optimized control algorithms to deliver rapid tactile feedback while maintaining minimal power consumption. Key design features include cost-effective piezoelectric actuators, microcontroller-based control systems, and energy-efficient operation protocols. Performance evaluation demonstrates refresh rates significantly faster than conventional Braille displays, with power consumption reduced by over 60% compared to existing commercial solutions. The device architecture supports multiple connectivity options and real-time text-to-Braille conversion, making it accessible for educational and professional applications. This work addresses critical barriers to Braille technology adoption through significant cost reduction and improved functionality.

  4. ICECE
    Impact of Doping and Defects on Thermal Transport of Monolayer GaN Nanoribbons: A Molecular Dynamics Simulation Study
    Md Zesun Ahmed Mia, Nazmus Saadat As-Saquib, Mehedi Hasan Himel, and 2 more authors
    In 2024 13th International Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering (ICECE), 2024
    Abs Bib

    This study employs large-scale molecular dynamics simulations to investigate the impact of doping concentrations and structural defects on thermal transport properties of monolayer gallium nitride (GaN) nanoribbons. Using the Green-Kubo method and non-equilibrium molecular dynamics techniques, we systematically analyze thermal conductivity variations as functions of dopant type, concentration, and defect density. Results reveal that silicon doping significantly reduces thermal conductivity due to phonon scattering mechanisms, while vacancy defects create localized thermal resistance hotspots. The study identifies optimal doping concentrations for thermal management applications in nanoelectronic devices. Phonon dispersion analysis reveals frequency-dependent scattering mechanisms responsible for observed thermal transport phenomena. These findings provide critical insights for designing GaN-based nanodevices with tailored thermal properties for applications in high-frequency electronics and thermal management systems.

  1. ISIEA
    Study of 3-nm Cylindrical GAAFETs with Variations in High-k Dielectric Gate-oxide Materials
    KM Ashraful Hoque Fahim, Md Jubair Hasan Khalid, Md Zesun Ahmed Mia, and 1 more author
    In 2022 IEEE Symposium on Industrial Electronics & Applications (ISIEA), 2022
    Abs Bib

    This work investigates the electrical performance characteristics of 3-nm node cylindrical Gate-All-Around Field-Effect Transistors (GAAFETs) with systematic variations in high-k dielectric gate-oxide materials. Using TCAD simulations, we analyze device performance metrics including threshold voltage, subthreshold swing, drain-induced barrier lowering, and on/off current ratios for different dielectric materials including HfO2, ZrO2, and Al2O3. The study reveals that higher dielectric constants enable better electrostatic control and reduced short-channel effects, while material-specific interface properties significantly influence device performance. Results demonstrate that optimized high-k dielectrics can achieve superior performance metrics essential for next-generation semiconductor technology nodes. The analysis provides critical insights for selecting appropriate gate-oxide materials in advanced GAAFET architectures for enhanced device scaling and performance optimization.

  2. ICO
    DCNN-LSTM based audio classification combining multiple feature engineering and data augmentation techniques
    Md Moinul Islam, Monjurul Haque, Saiful Islam, and 2 more authors
    In Intelligent Computing & Optimization: Proceedings of the 4th International Conference on Intelligent Computing and Optimization 2021 (ICO2021) 3, 2022
    Abs Bib

    This paper proposes a novel hybrid deep learning architecture combining Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNN) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks for robust audio classification. The approach integrates multiple advanced feature engineering techniques including Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients, spectral centroid, zero-crossing rate, and chroma features to capture comprehensive audio characteristics. Data augmentation strategies including time-stretching, pitch-shifting, and noise injection are employed to enhance model generalization and robustness. The DCNN component extracts spatial features from spectrograms while the LSTM layer captures temporal dependencies in audio sequences. Experimental evaluation on benchmark audio datasets demonstrates significant improvements in classification accuracy compared to traditional machine learning approaches and individual deep learning models. The proposed method achieves superior performance across diverse audio categories with enhanced noise robustness and computational efficiency.

  1. ICCIT
    Irfd: A feature engineering based ensemble classification for detecting electricity fraud in traditional meters
    Md Zesun Ahmed Mia, Md Moinul Islam, Monjurul Haque, and 2 more authors
    In 2021 24th International Conference on Computer and Information Technology (ICCIT), 2021
    Abs Bib

    This paper introduces IRFD (Intelligent Robust Fraud Detection), a comprehensive framework for detecting electricity fraud in traditional meter systems using advanced feature engineering and ensemble classification techniques. The proposed approach addresses critical challenges in power grid security by developing a robust machine learning pipeline that analyzes consumption patterns, temporal behaviors, and anomaly indicators. Key innovations include: (1) novel feature extraction methods from historical consumption data, (2) multi-scale temporal analysis for detecting irregular usage patterns, (3) ensemble classification combining random forests, gradient boosting, and support vector machines, and (4) real-time fraud detection capabilities with high accuracy and low false positive rates. Extensive validation on real-world power consumption datasets demonstrates detection accuracies exceeding 95% while maintaining computational efficiency suitable for large-scale deployment. The IRFD framework provides utilities with an effective tool for reducing revenue losses and improving grid security.
